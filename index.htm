PORTAL

With this site I would like to talk about one of my favorite game series, Portal.

Portal is a 2007 puzzle-platformer developed by VALVE, later they would go on to create Portal 2 which was released in 2011. I loved both Portal games growing up, it was fun to go through all the puzzles and learn all the lore and secrets, the aesthetics and style of the game is also something that really pulled me in and have inspired me in my own work.


The Game

Portal is a puzzle-platformer where you play as a woman named Chell who awakes from stasis to find herself trapped in the Aperture Science facility, here you are tasked by the facilities AI, GLaDOS, with solving an array of test with the promise of cake and freedom at the end. Throughout the tests, you slowly learn the mechanics of the game by solving physics based puzzles and more specifically, how to use the Portal Gun with these puzzles. Pushing boxes onto buttons, avoiding turrets and other deadly situations, making portals in the correct places to get around and successfully complete each puzzle. Each test becomes more difficult than the last as you and Chell both slowly learn that not everything is well in the facility and there is no prize nor freedom at the end of the testing chambers. Using everything learned from the tests, you must find a way to escape before GLaDOS finishes you off just as she did with the other test subjects.

Narrative Arc

The exposition of Portal begins with you, as Chell, waking up in a relaxation vault from stasis inside the Aperture Science facility. After being welcomed and briefed by the AI of the facility, GLaDOS, you are tasked with completing a set of tests with the promise of cake at the end as well as a supposed freedom. Rising action starts as you make your way through each test, it becomes clear that something is quite wrong with the facility, devoid of any other humans and strange scribblings hidden behind walls reveal that GLaDOS is actually a major threat, a rogue AI with a deadly past. Once you finally complete the test, GLaDOS immediately tries to dispose of you by dumping you into an incinerator, but luckily with the help of the Portal Gun you can escape, no longer trapped by test chambers and free to roam in the inner workings of the facility. The climax of the story would be when you eventually make your way to GLaDOS' lair where you face down against her as she tries to kill you, during this fight, you must hit GLaDOS with all the rockets she sends your way and once you do, she will drop her personality cores. You must grab all the personality cores that fall from GLaDOS and throw them in the incinerator to destroy them and defeat GLaDOS.

The Ending

At the end, once GLaDOS is finally defeated, a strange portal seems to appear in the roof that pulls both you and GLaDOS in, then everything goes white. Once more you awake but now you find yourself outside in some parking lot above ground, not longer trapped in the facility and surrounded by all the wreckage and debris from your battle. Before you can even get up, a voice is heard and something slowly begins to drags you back right into the facility as the screen fades to black.

Conclusion and Reflection

It’s hard to really put into words or fully describe why I like these games so much, I had initially found Portal by watching a fan-made music video for the end credits song to the second game “Want You Gone”, I thought the song was really good as well as the visuals, it had instantly drew me in and from there I just started watching more and more videos about the game until I had finally got it for myself. I loved playing it, even when I got stuck or kept failing I kept wanting to play more and more. I think this ties into what Lindley was saying about how even despite any frustration or anger we may feel while doing a puzzle, we end up going back to it and trying again (pg. 437), drawn in by the challenge and the desire to “prove” ourselves in our own minds. Whatever frustration I felt while playing Portal only made me want to try harder, I have a lot of fond memories of the games and still love them to this day.
